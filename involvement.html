Alpha Phi Omega Service Fraternity

January 2023 - Present

Alpha Phi Omega (APO) is the nation's largest co-ed service fraternity. Our goal is to provide service to our campus, community, fraternity, and nation while exemplifying our cardinal principles of leadership, friendship, and service. Since being initiated in January of 2023, I have accumulated over 180 hours of service.

As a brother in APO, I was elected to be the Spirit Chair for the Fall 2023 semester. With this, I gathered and shared positive news and significant feats of leadership, friendship, and service from both brothers and pledges to be shared with the chapter; I announced the news and upcoming birthdays to the chapter; and I planned and hosted Spirit Week, giving the brothers a chance to participate in fun and meaningful activities while becoming closer as brothers.

I had the privilege of serving as an Assistant to the Fellowship Vice President for the Spring 2024 semester. In this role, I assisted the Fellowship VP in planning and hosting fellowship events, allowing brothers to grow closer as a chapter; I assisted in planning the end-of-semester formal, an event to welcome the new brothers to the chapter.

Diversity Club

February 2022 - January 2023

The purpose of the Diversity Club is to provide High Point University students a safe space that promotes understanding and appreciation for people of various backgrounds. Our club takes a stand against prejudice and hatred and cultivates an environment where students have a voice to respectfully discuss diverse topics including the cultural identifier such as: age, gender, religion/spirituality, nationality, ethnicity/race, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, and ability/disability.

I served as the Social Media Chair for the duration of my time in the organization. In this role, it was my responsibility to create infographics and Instagram posts that promoted our weekly meetings, as well as any events we collaborated with other High Point organizations to host.
